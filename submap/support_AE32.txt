OVERALL BEST SOURCE
- Bock, Darrell L. *Acts.* Grand Rapids: Baker Academic, 2007.
  Why: Bock provides a comprehensive and accessible commentary that emphasizes the theological and historical context of Acts, making it an essential resource for understanding its distinctives.

DISTINCTIVES & SUPPORT
1. The theme of the Holy Spirit's empowerment for witness.
   - Stott, John. *The Spirit, the Church, and the World: The Message of Acts.* Downers Grove, IL: InterVarsity Press, 1990.
     Why: Stott explores the role of the Holy Spirit in the early church, highlighting its significance in the mission of the apostles.
   - Wright, N.T. *Acts for Everyone, Part 1: Chapters 1-12.* London: Society for Promoting Christian Knowledge, 2008.
     Why: Wright emphasizes the transformative power of the Spirit in the lives of believers and the church's mission.

2. The inclusion of Gentiles into the covenant community.
   - Horsley, Richard A. *Paul and Empire: Religion and Power in Roman Imperial Society.* Trinity Press International, 1997.
     Why: Horsley discusses the socio-political implications of Gentile inclusion as a radical shift in the early church's identity.
   - Witherington, Ben III. *The Acts of the Apostles: A Socio-Rhetorical Commentary.* Grand Rapids: Eerdmans, 1998.
     Why: Witherington provides insights into the cultural context of Gentile inclusion and its theological ramifications.

3. The portrayal of Paul as a pivotal figure in the mission to the Gentiles.
   - Fitzmyer, Joseph A. *The Acts of the Apostles: A New Translation with Introduction and Commentary.* New Haven: Yale University Press, 1998.
     Why: Fitzmyer offers a detailed analysis of Paul's role and the theological implications of his mission.
   - Longenecker, Richard N. *The Acts of the Apostles.* Grand Rapids: Eerdmans, 1995.
     Why: Longenecker examines Paul's missionary journeys and their significance for the spread of the gospel.

4. The narrative structure reflecting the transition from Jerusalem to Rome.
   - Talbert, Charles H. *Reading Acts: A Literary and Theological Commentary on the Acts of the Apostles.* Macon, GA: Smyth & Helwys Publishing, 2005.
     Why: Talbert analyzes the literary structure of Acts, emphasizing the geographical and thematic movement from Jerusalem to Rome.
   - Marshall, I. Howard. *The Acts of the Apostles.* Grand Rapids: Eerdmans, 1980.
     Why: Marshall discusses the narrative flow and its implications for understanding the early church's mission.

5. The significance of the temple as a symbol of God's presence.
   - Johnson, Luke Timothy. *The Acts of the Apostles.* Collegeville, MN: Liturgical Press, 1992.
     Why: Johnson explores the theological significance of the temple in Acts, particularly in relation to the new community of believers.
   - Wright, N.T. *Simply Jesus: A New Vision of Who He Was, What He Did, and Why He Matters.* New York: HarperOne, 2011.
     Why: Wright connects the temple's role in Acts to the broader narrative of Jesus' mission and the establishment of the new covenant community.

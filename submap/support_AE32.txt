Submap Bibliographic Support — Acts AIS

OVERALL BEST SOURCE
- Baylis, Charles P. “The Book of Acts.” *BiblicalStory.* 2015. http://thebiblicalstory.org/baylis/wp-content/uploads/2015/08/actslec081315Web.pdf.  
  Why: This source provides a comprehensive analysis of the themes, structure, and theological implications of Acts, making it essential for understanding the book's unique contributions.

DISTINCTIVES & SUPPORT
1. The theme of the "new Temple" as a community of believers.
   - Bock, Darrell L. *Acts.* Baker Exegetical Commentary on the New Testament. Grand Rapids: Baker Academic, 2007.  
     Why: Bock explores the significance of the Temple in Acts, emphasizing the transition from the physical Temple to the community of believers as the new dwelling place of God.
   - Wright, N. T. *Acts for Everyone, Part 1: Chapters 1-12.* SPCK, 2008.  
     Why: Wright discusses the implications of the new Temple concept in the context of early Christian identity and mission.

2. The inclusion of Gentiles as a central narrative arc.
   - Witherington, Ben III. *The Acts of the Apostles: A Socio-Rhetorical Commentary.* Grand Rapids: Eerdmans, 1998.  
     Why: Witherington provides insights into the socio-rhetorical context of Gentile inclusion, highlighting its significance in the early church.
   - Marshall, I. Howard. *The Acts of the Apostles.* Tyndale New Testament Commentaries. Downers Grove: InterVarsity Press, 1980.  
     Why: Marshall examines the theological implications of Gentile inclusion and its roots in the Jewish faith.

3. The role of the Holy Spirit in empowering the apostles.
   - Keener, Craig S. *Acts: An Exegetical Commentary, Volume 1: 1:1-2:47.* Grand Rapids: Baker Academic, 2012.  
     Why: Keener's detailed commentary emphasizes the transformative role of the Holy Spirit in the early church's mission.
   - Stott, John. *The Spirit, the Church, and the World: The Message of Acts.* Downers Grove: InterVarsity Press, 1990.  
     Why: Stott articulates the essential role of the Holy Spirit in guiding and empowering the church's mission.

4. The portrayal of Paul as a pivotal figure in the mission to the Gentiles.
   - Fitzmyer, Joseph A. *The Acts of the Apostles: A New Translation with Introduction and Commentary.* New Haven: Yale University Press, 1998.  
     Why: Fitzmyer provides a thorough examination of Paul's role and significance in the narrative of Acts.
   - Longenecker, Richard N. *The Acts of the Apostles.* The Expositor's Bible Commentary. Grand Rapids: Zondervan, 1981.  
     Why: Longenecker discusses Paul's missionary journeys and theological contributions within the broader narrative of Acts.

5. The conflict between the early church and Jewish authorities.
   - Horsley, Richard A. *Paul and Empire: Religion and Power in Roman Imperial Society.* Trinity Press International, 1997.  
     Why: Horsley analyzes the socio-political dynamics between the early church and Jewish authorities, providing context for their conflicts.
   - Johnson, Luke Timothy. *The Acts of the Apostles.* Sacra Pagina Series. Collegeville: Liturgical Press, 1992.  
     Why: Johnson offers insights into the nature of the conflicts faced by the apostles and the implications for the early church's identity.

6. The narrative structure emphasizing the spread of the gospel.
   - Wright, Christopher J. H. *The Mission of God: Unlocking the Bible's Grand Narrative.* Downers Grove: InterVarsity Press, 2006.  
     Why: Wright discusses the overarching narrative of Acts as part of God's mission, highlighting the spread of the gospel as a central theme.
   - Peterson, David. *The Acts of the Apostles.* The Pillar New Testament Commentary. Grand Rapids: Eerdmans, 2009.  
     Why: Peterson analyzes the narrative flow of Acts, emphasizing the strategic expansion of the church's mission.

7. The significance of eyewitness testimony in the narrative.
   - Bauckham, Richard. *Jesus and the Eyewitnesses: The Gospels as Eyewitness Testimony.* Grand Rapids: Eerdmans, 2006.  
     Why: Bauckham's work on eyewitness testimony provides a framework for understanding the historical reliability of Acts.
   - Blomberg, Craig L. *The Historical Reliability of the Gospels.* Downers Grove: InterVarsity Press, 1987.  
     Why: Blomberg discusses the implications of eyewitness accounts for the credibility of the narratives in Acts.

8. The theological implications of suffering and persecution.
   - Wright, N. T. *Simply Jesus: A New Vision of Who He Was, What He Did, and Why He Matters.* New York: HarperOne, 2011.  
     Why: Wright addresses the theme of suffering in the context of Jesus' mission and its continuation in Acts.
   - Green, Michael. *Evangelism in the Early Church.* Grand Rapids: Eerdmans, 2003.  
     Why: Green explores how suffering and persecution shaped the evangelistic efforts of the early church as depicted in Acts.

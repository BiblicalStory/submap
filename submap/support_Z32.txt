Submap Bibliographic Support — Luke AIS

OVERALL BEST SOURCE
- Bock, Darrell L., and Benjamin I. Simpson. _Jesus According to Scripture_. Grand Rapids, MI: Baker Academic, 2017.
  Why: This comprehensive work provides an in-depth analysis of Luke's theological contributions and narrative structure, making it an essential resource for understanding the distinctives of the Gospel of Luke.

DISTINCTIVES & SUPPORT
1. Emphasis on the marginalized and outcasts.
   - Bock, Darrell L. _Luke: 1:1–9:50_. Grand Rapids, MI: Baker Academic, 1996.
     Why: Bock highlights Luke's unique focus on social outcasts and the marginalized, illustrating how Jesus' ministry reaches those often overlooked by society.
   - Green, Joel B. _The Gospel of Luke_. Grand Rapids, MI: Eerdmans, 1997.
     Why: Green discusses how Luke's narrative prioritizes the experiences of the poor and marginalized, emphasizing the inclusivity of Jesus' message.

2. The role of women in Jesus' ministry.
   - Johnson, Luke Timothy. _The Gospel of Luke_. Collegeville, MN: Liturgical Press, 1991.
     Why: Johnson explores the significant roles women play in Luke's Gospel, showcasing their involvement in Jesus' ministry and the early church.
   - Schaberg, Patricia. _The Resurrection of Mary Magdalene: Jesus and the First Woman Apostle_. New York: Continuum, 2002.
     Why: Schaberg examines the portrayal of women in Luke, particularly Mary Magdalene, and argues for their critical roles in the resurrection narrative.

3. Theological emphasis on forgiveness and repentance.
   - Fitzmyer, Joseph A. _The Gospel According to Luke I–IX_. New York: Doubleday, 1981.
     Why: Fitzmyer provides a thorough analysis of Luke's teachings on forgiveness and repentance, highlighting their centrality to Jesus' message.
   - Nolland, John. _Luke 1–9:20_. Word Biblical Commentary. Dallas, TX: Word Books, 1989.
     Why: Nolland discusses the importance of forgiveness in Luke's narrative, emphasizing how it shapes the identity of Jesus and his followers.

4. The significance of the Holy Spirit.
   - Marshall, I. Howard. _The Gospel of Luke: A Commentary on the Greek Text_. Exeter: Paternoster Press, 1978.
     Why: Marshall examines the role of the Holy Spirit in Luke's Gospel, noting its influence on Jesus' ministry and the empowerment of the apostles.
   - Horrell, David G. _The Holy Spirit in the Gospel of Luke_. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 1994.
     Why: Horrell provides a focused study on the theme of the Holy Spirit in Luke, detailing its implications for understanding the Gospel's message.

5. The eschatological perspective of the "little flock."
   - Bock, Darrell L. _Luke 9:51–24:53_. Grand Rapids, MI: Baker Academic, 1996.
     Why: Bock discusses the eschatological themes in Luke, particularly the concept of the "little flock" and their role in the coming kingdom.
   - Wright, N. T. _Jesus and the Victory of God_. Minneapolis, MN: Fortress Press, 1996.
     Why: Wright explores the eschatological dimensions of Jesus' ministry in Luke, emphasizing the hope and expectation of the "little flock" in the face of adversity.

**Title:** **The Literary Argument of the Book of Psalms (AIS)**

The Book of Psalms presents a theological narrative where God's covenant faithfulness, embodied in the concept of lovingkindness, underpins Israel's hope despite the failures of its kings. Central to this narrative is the Davidic Covenant, which anticipates a future King-Priest from the line of David who will restore God's kingdom and lead a new Exodus to Zion. The Psalter explores themes of righteousness, the significance of Zion, and the enduring nature of God's promises, culminating in the expectation of a messianic figure who will fulfill these divine purposes.

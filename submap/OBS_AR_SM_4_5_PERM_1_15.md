**Title:** **The Literary Argument of the Book of Psalms (AIS)**

The Book of Psalms articulates a theological narrative centered on God's covenant faithfulness and the anticipated Davidic King, who will lead His people to a new Exodus and establish His eternal kingdom. It emphasizes key themes such as lovingkindness, righteousness, and the enduring significance of Zion, while portraying the struggles of David and Israel amidst sin and adversity. The Psalter ultimately points to a future hope in the promised Seed of David, who embodies God's redemptive purposes despite the apparent failures of human kingship.

## Summary
The Argument of Matthew presents a theological interpretation of the Gospel of Matthew, emphasizing the role of Jesus as the virgin-born King who comes to save Israel and establish a new ecclesia through a remnant. The narrative unfolds in a structured plot that includes the setting of Jesus' ministry, the conflict of rejection by the Pharisees, and the rising action characterized by parables that instruct the disciples during a delay period before the kingdom's arrival. The climax reveals Jesus as the greater Moses, who enacts a new Exodus, culminating in His resurrection and the commissioning of the apostles to spread the gospel to all nations. This work highlights the convergence of Old Testament promises in the figure of the Shepherd-King and the significance of the remnant in the fulfillment of God's plan.

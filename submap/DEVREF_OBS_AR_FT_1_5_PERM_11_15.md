## Summary
The Argument of Luke, as presented by S. Baylis and GPT-5, explores the narrative framework of the Gospel of Luke, emphasizing Jesus' role as the savior who identifies with unworthy Israel to bring forgiveness through the apostles to all nations. The narrative unfolds through a series of conflicts, rising actions, and resolutions, culminating in the resurrection and the apostles' empowerment to spread the message of forgiveness. Additionally, the text discusses eschatological themes in Luke, highlighting the immediacy of the Son of Man's return and the tribulation period, which together shape the expectations of believers during the inter-advent age.

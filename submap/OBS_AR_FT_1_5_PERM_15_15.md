**Title:** (AIS) Argument of Matthew

The Argument of Matthew presents a theological interpretation of the Gospel of Matthew, emphasizing the role of Jesus as the virgin-born King who comes to save Israel and establish a new ecclesia through a remnant. This remnant, trained by Jesus, is tasked with spreading the gospel to all nations despite opposition and rejection from the Pharisees and the broader nation of Israel. The narrative structure highlights key themes such as the fulfillment of Old Testament prophecies, the significance of parables in teaching about the kingdom, and the eventual triumph of Jesus as the resurrected King who sends His apostles to disciple all nations. The work underscores the continuity between the Old Testament promises and their realization in the New Testament through Jesus and His followers.

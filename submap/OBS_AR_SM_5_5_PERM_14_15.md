**Title:** **The Literary Argument of the Book of Joshua**

The Book of Joshua emphasizes the importance of humility and reliance on God's holiness rather than human strength. It presents the Word of the LORD as the true Image of God, which Israel must follow, symbolized through the dynamics of the Ban and the sacrificial love embodied in the narrative. The text illustrates the tension between Israel's failures and God's unwavering faithfulness, highlighting that true holiness is not inherent in Israel but is found in the Seed that God provides. The literary structure of Joshua reinforces these themes through key events and characters, ultimately portraying the necessity of acknowledging God's holiness for unity and peace.

DISTINCTIVES & SUPPORT
1. Jesus as the Isaianic Servant
   - Kaiser, Walter C., Jr. “Exodus.” In _Expositor’s Bible Commentary: Genesis–Leviticus (Revised Edition)_, edited by Tremper Longman III and David E. Garland, vol. 1. Grand Rapids, MI: Zondervan, 2008.
     Why: This commentary discusses the fulfillment of Isaiah's prophecies in the context of Jesus' ministry, emphasizing His role as the Servant.
   - Baylis, Charles P. “The Book of Mark.” _BiblicalStory_. 2015. http://thebiblicalstory.org/baylis/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/MarkCls061015.pdf
     Why: Baylis highlights the introduction of Jesus as the Servant and its implications for understanding His authority and mission.

2. The Emergence of a Spirit-Empowered Remnant
   - Brisco, Thomas V. _Holman Bible Atlas_. Nashville, TN: Broadman & Holman, 1998.
     Why: This atlas provides geographical and cultural context that supports the narrative of a remnant emerging in response to Israel's rejection.
   - Baylis, Charles P. “The Book of Mark.” _BiblicalStory_. 2015. http://thebiblicalstory.org/baylis/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/MarkCls061015.pdf
     Why: The source discusses the formation of a new family of believers as a response to the rejection of Jesus by the leaders.

3. The Corruption of the Temple and New Community Formation
   - Kaiser, Walter C., Jr. “Exodus.” In _Expositor’s Bible Commentary: Genesis–Leviticus (Revised Edition)_, edited by Tremper Longman III and David E. Garland, vol. 1. Grand Rapids, MI: Zondervan, 2008.
     Why: This commentary addresses the significance of the temple's corruption and the establishment of a new community in light of Jesus' teachings.
   - Baylis, Charles P. “The Book of Mark.” _BiblicalStory_. 2015. http://thebiblicalstory.org/baylis/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/MarkCls061015.pdf
     Why: Baylis emphasizes the transition from the old order to the new community formed by Jesus' followers.

4. The Role of Discipleship and Grace
   - Brisco, Thomas V. _Holman Bible Atlas_. Nashville, TN: Broadman & Holman, 1998.
     Why: This source provides insights into the geographical and cultural aspects of discipleship in the Gospel of Mark.
   - Baylis, Charles P. “The Book of Mark.” _BiblicalStory_. 2015. http://thebiblicalstory.org/baylis/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/MarkCls061015.pdf
     Why: The source discusses the failures of the disciples and the necessity of grace in their mission.

5. Eschatological Themes in Mark
   - Baylis, Charles P. “The Book of Mark.” _BiblicalStory_. 2015. http://thebiblicalstory.org/baylis/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/MarkCls061015.pdf
     Why: This source provides a detailed examination of the eschatological framework in Mark, particularly in relation to the end times.
   - Kaiser, Walter C., Jr. “Exodus.” In _Expositor’s Bible Commentary: Genesis–Leviticus (Revised Edition)_, edited by Tremper Longman III and David E. Garland, vol. 1. Grand Rapids, MI: Zondervan, 2008.
     Why: This commentary offers insights into the prophetic elements that relate to eschatology in the context of Mark's narrative.

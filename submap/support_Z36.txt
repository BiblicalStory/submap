Submap Bibliographic Support — Matthew AIS

OVERALL BEST SOURCE
- France, R. T. "The Gospel of Matthew." New International Commentary on the New Testament. Eerdmans, 2007.
  Why: France's commentary is widely regarded for its thorough exegesis, clarity, and engagement with both the text and its theological implications, making it an essential resource for understanding Matthew's distinctive contributions.

DISTINCTIVES & SUPPORT
1. Jesus as the Fulfillment of the Old Testament
   - Blomberg, Craig L. "Matthew." New American Commentary. Broadman & Holman, 1992.
     Why: Blomberg emphasizes how Matthew presents Jesus as the fulfillment of Jewish prophecy, providing a comprehensive analysis of the Old Testament references throughout the Gospel.
   - Hagner, Donald A. "Matthew 1-13." Word Biblical Commentary. Thomas Nelson, 1993.
     Why: Hagner discusses the fulfillment motif in detail, illustrating how Matthew's narrative structure aligns with Old Testament expectations.

2. The Emphasis on the Remnant
   - Nolland, John. "The Gospel of Matthew: A Commentary on the Greek Text." New International Greek Testament Commentary. Eerdmans, 2005.
     Why: Nolland explores the theme of the remnant in Matthew, highlighting its significance in the context of Israel's rejection and the establishment of the new ecclesia.
   - Luz, Ulrich. "Matthew 1-7: A Commentary." Hermeneia. Fortress Press, 2007.
     Why: Luz provides insight into the remnant concept, particularly in relation to Jesus' teachings and the formation of the church.

3. The Kingdom of Heaven and Its Parables
   - Keener, Craig S. "The Gospel of Matthew: A Socio-Rhetorical Commentary." Eerdmans, 2009.
     Why: Keener's work offers a socio-rhetorical perspective on the parables, emphasizing their role in teaching about the nature of the kingdom and the delay before its full realization.
   - Davies, W. D., and Dale C. Allison. "A Critical and Exegetical Commentary on the Gospel According to Saint Matthew." International Critical Commentary. T&T Clark, 1988.
     Why: This commentary provides a detailed analysis of the parables, discussing their theological implications and how they reflect the kingdom's characteristics.

4. The Olivet Discourse and Eschatology
   - Carson, D. A. "Matthew." Expositor's Bible Commentary. Zondervan, 1984.
     Why: Carson's commentary on the Olivet Discourse is particularly insightful, addressing the eschatological themes and their relevance for the church's mission during the delay.
   - Wright, N. T. "Jesus and the Victory of God." Fortress Press, 1996.
     Why: Wright discusses the Olivet Discourse in the context of Jesus' mission and the expectations of the kingdom, providing a robust theological framework for understanding Matthew's eschatology.

5. The Role of the Church in Matthew
   - Hagner, Donald A. "Matthew 14-28." Word Biblical Commentary. Thomas Nelson, 1995.
     Why: Hagner examines the ecclesiological implications of Matthew, particularly in the context of the Great Commission and the church's mission.
   - Guelich, Robert A. "Mark 1-8:26." Word Biblical Commentary. Thomas Nelson, 1989.
     Why: While focused on Mark, Guelich's insights into the early church's formation and mission provide valuable parallels to Matthew's ecclesiology.

6. The Theme of Righteousness
   - Stassen, Glen H., and David P. Gushee. "Kingdom Ethics: Following Jesus in Contemporary Context." IVP Academic, 2003.
     Why: This work discusses the ethical implications of righteousness in Matthew, emphasizing its relevance for discipleship and community life.
   - Hays, Richard B. "The Moral Vision of the New Testament: Community, Cross, New Creation." HarperOne, 1996.
     Why: Hays explores the theme of righteousness in the New Testament, with specific references to Matthew's teachings on ethical living.

7. The Role of Women in Matthew
   - Bock, Darrell L. "Luke." Baker Exegetical Commentary on the New Testament. Baker Academic, 1996.
     Why: Bock's commentary includes discussions on the portrayal of women in the Gospels, including Matthew, highlighting their significance in the narrative.
   - Schüssler Fiorenza, Elisabeth. "In Memory of Her: A Feminist Theological Reconstruction of Christian Origins." Crossroad, 1983.
     Why: This foundational work addresses the role of women in the early church, with implications for understanding their representation in Matthew.

8. The Use of Fulfillment Quotations
   - Blomberg, Craig L. "Jesus and the Gospels: An Introduction and Survey." Broadman & Holman, 1997.
     Why: Blomberg discusses the significance of fulfillment quotations in Matthew, providing a framework for understanding their theological implications.
   - Green, Joel B. "The Gospel of Luke." New International Commentary on the New Testament. Eerdmans, 1997.
     Why: Green's comparative analysis of fulfillment in the Gospels offers insights into Matthew's unique approach to Old Testament citations.

Overall, the sources selected provide a comprehensive understanding of Matthew's distinctive contributions, addressing themes such as fulfillment, the remnant, the kingdom, and ecclesiology, all of which are central to the Gospel's message.

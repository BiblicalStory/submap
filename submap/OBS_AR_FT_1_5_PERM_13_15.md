**Title:** (AIS) Argument of Matthew

The Argument of Matthew presents a theological narrative where Jesus, depicted as the virgin-born King, comes to save Israel, but faces rejection from the nation. A remnant of believers is preserved and tasked with spreading the gospel to all nations. The plot unfolds through various discourses and parables that illustrate the nature of the kingdom and the role of the remnant during a delay period before the kingdom's full realization. The Olivet Discourse serves as a climactic moment, emphasizing readiness for tribulation and the eventual restoration of Israel, culminating in the remnant's mission to disciple all nations.

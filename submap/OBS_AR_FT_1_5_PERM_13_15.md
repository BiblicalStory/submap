**Title:** (AIS) Argument of Matthew

The argument of Matthew presents Jesus as the virgin-born King who comes to save Israel from sin, establishing a remnant that will spread the gospel to all nations. The narrative unfolds through a structured plot that includes Jesus' introduction, the tension of rejection, rising action through parables, and the climax of His sacrificial death and resurrection. Matthew emphasizes the fulfillment of Old Testament promises and the establishment of a new ecclesia, highlighting themes of righteousness, mercy, and the delay before the kingdom's full realization. The Olivet Discourse serves as a prophetic framework for understanding the delay and the eventual restoration of Israel.

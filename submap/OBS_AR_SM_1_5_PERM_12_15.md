**Title:** **The Literary Argument of the Book of Joshua**

The Book of Joshua emphasizes the importance of humility and reliance on God's holiness rather than human strength. It presents the Word of the LORD as the true Image of God, embodying His character and love, while the concept of the Ban illustrates God's prerogative to judge sin and preserve holiness. The narrative contrasts figures like Rahab and Achan to highlight that salvation is rooted in God's holiness, not human merit. Ultimately, the text underscores that Israel's identity and unity are found in acknowledging God's holiness, which is central to their covenant relationship.

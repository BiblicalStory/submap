**Title:** **The Literary Argument of the Book of Joshua**

The Book of Joshua emphasizes the importance of humility and reliance on God's holiness rather than human strength. It presents the Word of the LORD as the true Image of God, highlighting the dynamic of the Ban, which signifies God's judgment and preservation of holiness. The narrative illustrates that Israel's victories stem from God's faithfulness, not their righteousness, and underscores the need for a Greater Moses to fulfill God's mission. Key themes include the contrast between self-righteousness and faith, the significance of the altar as a witness to God's holiness, and the acknowledgment that Israel's identity is rooted in divine grace.

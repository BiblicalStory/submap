**Title:** **The Literary Argument of the Book of Joshua**

The Book of Joshua emphasizes that Israel's relationship with God is defined by humility and reliance on His holiness rather than their own strength. The narrative illustrates the concept of the 'Ban,' which signifies God's authority over holiness and judgment, and it contrasts the faith of figures like Rahab with the failures of Israel's leaders. The text argues that true holiness is embodied in God's self-giving love, as seen through the actions of the ark and the altar of witness. Ultimately, the message conveys that Israel's identity and unity stem from acknowledging God's holiness, not from their self-righteousness.

**Title:** **The Literary Argument of the Book of Joshua**

The Book of Joshua emphasizes the importance of humility and reliance on God's holiness rather than human strength. It presents the Word of the LORD as the true Image of God, highlighting the significance of the ban as God's prerogative to judge sin and maintain holiness. The narrative illustrates that Israel's victories stem from God's faithfulness, not their righteousness, and underscores the need for a Greater Moses to fulfill God's mission. Key figures like Rahab and Achan exemplify the contrast between faith and self-righteousness, reinforcing the theme that God's holiness is central to Israel's identity and unity.

**Title:** Narrative Framework

The Argument of Luke presents a narrative framework where Jesus, as the ultimate Israelite, identifies with the unworthy to offer forgiveness through the New Covenant. The story unfolds through various stages, highlighting Israel's pride and rejection of forgiveness, contrasting with Jesus' humility and mercy. The eschatological themes in Luke emphasize both the immediacy of the Son of Man's return and the tribulation period, urging believers to maintain an expectation of deliverance. This framework culminates in the apostles receiving the Spirit's power to spread the message of forgiveness to all nations.
